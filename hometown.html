City of Samarinda

Samarinda is the capital city of the East Kalimantan province in Indonesia. Located on the island of Borneo, it sits along the banks of the Mahakam River, one of the longest rivers in Indonesia. As a major port and commercial hub, Samarinda is renowned for its timber, coal mining, and oil industries, which contribute significantly to the local economy.

The city is rich in cultural heritage, blending the traditions of the indigenous Dayak people, Bugis, and Banjar communities, creating a diverse and vibrant society. Known for its traditional floating houses called "rumah lanting," Samarinda offers unique architecture that harmonizes with its riverine environment. Additionally, it serves as a gateway to the lush rainforests of Borneo, making it an ideal destination for those looking to explore the natural beauty and biodiversity of the region.

As Samarinda continues to grow, it maintains a balance between modernization and preserving its natural and cultural legacy, making it a fascinating place for both locals and visitors alike.
